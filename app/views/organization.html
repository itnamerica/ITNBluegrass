Board of Directors

Michael Smith

Board Chair

Michael Smith retired from the University of Kentucky after 15 years, having served as Executive Director of the university’s academic and enrichment programs for older adults (the Donovans and the OLLI), Assistant Dean for Evaluation and Planning in the College of Public Health, Associate Director of the Sanders-Brown Center on Aging, and Assistant Professor in the Department of Health Services Management. He has volunteered as a Board member and officer on non-profit boards, focusing on organizations dealing with aging and health.

Mike worked as a senior administrator of hospitals for 19 years, before coming to Kentucky as Executive Director the Alzheimer’s Association for Central and Eastern Kentucky (1999-2001). He has an M.H.A. (Master of Health Administration) from Indiana University and an M.A. and Ph.D. in philosophy from Loyola University of Chicago. In faculty appointments at Xavier University, University of Notre Dame, and East Tennessee State University he taught in areas of bioethics, humanities, and human resource management.

Before he retired, he worked for IBM Corporation and Lexmark International in senior management. In his free time, he enjoys sports and sports memorabilia from baseball and football to basketball and golf. Helen Kientz

Secretary

Helen is originally from the NJ/NY/Long Island area and is a graduate of the Katharine Gibbs School, New York. A past president of Lawrenceville, NJ, and Lexington, KY, Newcomers' Clubs, she is also a graduate of the Lexington Citizens' Police Academy. Her career path included volunteering as an officer of PTAs in the schools her daughter attended in Colorado and New Jersey, supervising Girl Scouts, and working as a Kelly Girl, at several Presbyterian churches, and in legal and medical offices.

Helen retired as the office manager of the Lexington office of the Greater Kentucky and Southern Indiana Chapter of the Alzheimer's Association. She has volunteered and been employed by the Alzheimer's Association for the past eighteen years in various capacities including development, volunteer coordinating, event planning, and serving on the Board of Directors after retirement. She continues her volunteering at the Association working in the Lexington office and serving on the Walk to End Alzheimer's committee (2015 will be her 17th Walk) and also is a volunteer driver for ITN.

Having worked with senior citizens for the past eighteen years, she has learned that one of the most dire needs for seniors to remain independent is transportation. ITN can fulfill that need if more people are aware of its existence. She is promoting ITN wherever and whenever she can!

Hazel Teichen

Board Member

Hazel worked for a hospice provider for 8 years in Chicago prior to relocating from Illinois to Kentucky in 2015. She considers it a blessing to work alongside a professional team of clinicians at Bluegrass Care Navigators for just over 3 years. As a Provider Liaison her primary responsibility is to connect with physicians, healthcare providers, and residents in the community to share information and educate on the many services available thru Bluegrass Care Navigators.

She has the highest job satisfaction when she is representing Bluegrass Care Navigators and presenting to a professional or a community group to educate on our services especially hospice care. As an advocate for the highest quality of life, personal dignity, and compassionate care that is the core of hospice, she works to correct myths about what hospice is or is not and shift the focus to the benefits that can be realized for patients, families and caregivers at a most personal time in their lives.

Hazel is the proud mom of her daughter Renee who lives in CT and her son Billy who lives in Brooklyn, NY. She has a beautiful cat named Macie Boots who brings joy, laughter, and calm to her life. Among other accomplishment she got her motorcycle license and Master’s Degree at the age of 58.

Charles Coulston

Board Member

Originally from central Indiana, Charles has lived in Lexington since 1982 when he came to work at Good Samaritan Hospital as a nuclear medicine technologist. In 1987, he became the program director of the associate of applied science degree program in nuclear medicine technology at Lexington Community College (now Bluegrass Community and Technical College). Teaching and community service were the emphases at the community college rather than research, so he has spent time volunteering not only for professional societies but also for local groups such as Ronald McDonald House, A Moveable Feast, and ITNB in the past 30 years. He is also very involved at St Michael's Episcopal Church having served on the vestry (board), nominating committee for clergy, sabbatical planning committee, and choir among other groups in the church.

Charles has two sons. Charles who teaches math at Elizabethtown Community and Technical College, and Robert who is the current chaplain at St Augustine's Chapel on UK's campus for the Episcopal/Lutheran Ministry. Charles and his partner Beth have his three grandchildren, Dottie, Kitty, and Gus.

In 2017, Charles retired from BCTC, and he now works as a nuclear medicine technologist again 1 1/2 days/week at Lexington Diagnostic Center and Open MRI. The remainder of his time is devoted to A Moveable Feast, ITNB, St Michael's and his grandchildren. He also enjoy traveling with a friend from Chicago. They just returned from a tour of Morocco, and last year traveled to England on the Queen Mary 2 and spent about 10 days traveling on the trains and buses to see some of the great cathedrals and hear the choirs in Bristol, Cardiff (Wales), Coventry, Lincoln, York, Durham, and Edinburgh (Scotland).

Kathleen Richardson

Board Member

Kathleen Richardson has been a librarian at Bluegrass Community & Technical College (formerly Lexington Community College) since 1989 and has nearly 30 years of experience in libraries. She credits her first library science course--an outstanding cataloging class with Liz Bishoff at Rosary College's Graduate School for Library and Information Science-- for sparking her interest in technical services.

Staff

Jennifer Gripshover

Executive Director

Jennifer Gripshover joined ITNBluegrass as Executive Director in March of 2018. Prior to ITN she had a 20 year career in education, including curriculum design, student affairs, records, academic administration, and management. She found her passion when she became Dean of a career college. Here she was able to help nontraditional students from under served populations to change their lives through education.

She later opened the ITT Technical Institute campus in Lexington in 2006 as its first College Director. She grew that campus to over 600 students and oversaw the addition of the Breckinridge School of Nursing.

Jennifer attends Ohavay Zion Synagogue where she is the Co-Chair of the Social Action Committee. She volunteers with Kentucky Refugee Ministries and Bluegrass Care Navigators. Her heart turned to senior causes when her parents moved into a senior living community. She saw how many challenges they faced in maintaining an active and meaningful life, so when she had the opportunity to enter the next phase of her career she committed herself to helping seniors.

Jennifer grew up in the Northeast and has lived overseas in France, Turkey and England. She has a Bachelors’ degree from Tufts University in International Relations and a Masters’ from Lesley College in Intercultural Training and Development. She came to Lexington 24 years ago after 2 years in England. Jennifer lives in Lexington with her husband, two sons, and two dogs.
